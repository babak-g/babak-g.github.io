## Difference between Veganism and WFPB

As someone mentioned on Reddit - vegans avoid of animal and animal byproducts, but can eat anything else, while WFPB (Whole Foods Plant Based) requires all foods to be non-refined and from plants.

Oil is a common example that differentiates the two. Vegans can cook with and consume oils, while WFPB don't because the oil process extracts other nutrients in the process of making it.

## Restrictive, but not

Social gatherings, quintessential cultural meals all revolve around foods that don't fit in WFPB. I find this a little difficult to digest (lol) because I do think there exists a community aspect in food. Restricting myself of these foods sounds difficult, but I do think there are benefits that involve not having to track. I've found that natural, whole foods to be a great way to eat all kinds of food. I think that not obstructing the state of the food provides immense benefits to my body and naturally balances everything consumed.

What I mean by this is that, there isn't much of a need to track macros/micros etc. because eating whole foods does a pretty good job at doing that itself. If meals revolve around eating different foods, I'll get pretty good diversity of nutrients I'd guess a fair share of the time.

On other lifestyles of eating, it can get overwhelming thinking about which foods have what nutrients, which requires a pretty strong foundation of knowledge to begin with. If I know nothing about nutrition, which I truthfully don't know much, I can just go to a grocery store and just whip something together without restriction of specific numbers.

I really like the idea of this, but I could do this in an animal diet as well.

## Animal

When I talk about my eating habits to anyone, the days I've prioritized meat in meals have made me feel the best. I had to be careful the other foods I ate alongside meat to capture the full spectrum of nutrients, but this worked for me given my activity level.

Although, I still  currently eat meat, I do think this is something I want to try and record for myself. 

As I am running a lot more in preparation for a marathon in April, I need a larger amount of carbs. Plants not only provide these, but also pack other essential nutrients as well.

I don't think anyone is a bad person for eating animals. I do think it'd be interesting to try out and consider. Hopefully, we'll try it out and record back at a later date.
