Eide Shoma Mobarak.

Been ~3 months since my last post, yet so much has happened. Let's recap:

- Turned 27
- Achilles Tendonitis from marathon training (1 month until it's showtime!)
- Finished Physical Therapy for my shoulder surgery back in September
- My cousin is so talented and had an amazing play in San Diego
- Got into Grad School
- Went Vegan Last Month
- Quarter Life Crisis / Lost Purpose
- Started Therapy
- Took Mental Health leave at work 

I feel so sad. I still haven't figured out what was the trigger, but my brain clicked. Waking up and feeling lost is such a terrible feeling. The funny part is I have no idea what I want to do in the future, nor have a correct direction. I got satisfaction in knowing what I don't want. 

I don't know if this whole vegan thing is making me so emotional, but I've never felt this depressed & anxious in my life. Starting January, I began to feel alone. Just so lonely. I felt like I changed so much that I drifted away from all my relationships. Anytime I wanted to talk to someone, I realized I had no one to call. It made me introspect that I lost myself of things that meant the most to me. This dark time, the people that honestly saved me were my parents.

I was having panic attacks almost everyday, and didn't know who to talk to. I ended up going to my parent's home everyday [not telling them what was going on with me, but deep down I knew they knew I wasn't okay]. Watching the Superbowl with them, going to mamanbozorg's birthday on a workday, it was sad seeing loved ones see me like this, but it clicked that I had everything I needed around me.

I felt like I was drowning and my parents jumped in, grabbed my hand, and brought me back to surface. I started to focus on what I needed and truly wanted in my life. Took mental health, felt very well supported by my mom, girlfriend, and therapist. 

The conversation with my manager was terrible that I was going on leave and very difficult. Even though my leave isn't approved yet, I can't stress enough it was one of the best decisions I've made. I don't know my future, but this felt so right. Truly, a pebble that weighed a ton lifted off my chest. That week was phenomenal and got to see my parents everyday, things were much better for me, but hard news followed, which is a conversation for another time.
